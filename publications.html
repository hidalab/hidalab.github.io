Research areas

My work sits at the intersection of human centered AI, health information, and the science of science. I study how people search for, interpret, and use information in a world shaped by digital platforms and AI.

AI literacy and education Health informatics and digital health Scientometrics and altmetrics Misinformation and social media
AI

AI literacy and education

How students, educators, and communities understand, adopt, and critically engage with AI systems in schools, universities, and libraries, with attention to equity and human agency.

AI literacy frameworks School libraries Higher education
Includes curriculum design for AI literacy in school libraries and global surveys of AI use in academia.
H

Health informatics and digital health

Digital platforms and AI in health information, including how patients, parents, and professionals encounter trustworthy information, misinformation, and decision support tools.

Digital health Health misinformation Social media data
Projects include work on eating disorder content on TikTok, COVID 19 misinformation, and cross cultural studies of misinformation drivers and coping strategies.
S

Scientometrics and altmetrics

Quantitative and mixed methods research on scholarly communication, including altmetrics, policy impact, and global inequalities in the visibility and use of scientific work.

Altmetrics Impact assessment Global inequalities
Includes Garfield Award winning work on web based indicators and ongoing collaboration on AI and research quality.
M

Misinformation, search, and platforms

How search engines, social media, and AI based tools shape exposure to misinformation, and how people navigate, resist, or appropriate these systems for learning and decision making.

Search behavior Misinformation Human AI interaction
Current work compares traditional keyword search with AI augmented search results and studies user trust and behavior.
