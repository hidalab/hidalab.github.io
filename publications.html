AI literacy and education

How learners, educators, and communities understand, adopt, and critically engage with AI tools across formal and informal learning environments.

AI literacy frameworks Libraries and schools Human centered AI
  • 2024. AI literacy in school libraries. Conceptual framework and design principles for integrating AI literacy into school library programs, with a focus on equity and resilience.
  • 2023. Understanding PhD students’ interactions with ChatGPT. Empirical study of how doctoral students evaluate, adapt, and critique generated content in research contexts.
  • 2022. From digital to AI literacies. Mapping continuities and gaps between long standing digital literacy work and emerging AI literacy agendas in libraries and information institutions.

Includes collaborative work with school librarians, instructional designers, and information literacy experts in multi site studies and curriculum design projects.

Health informatics and digital health

Intersections of digital platforms, data, and health information behavior, including the opportunities and risks of AI mediated health information.

Digital health Health misinformation Social media data
  • 2023. Eating disorder content and engagement on TikTok. Large scale study of content themes, information strategies, and user engagement around eating disorder videos, published in a leading medical journal.
  • 2022. COVID 19 misinformation across platforms. Comparative analysis of narrative strategies and misinformation patterns in health related content across multiple social media sites.
  • Ongoing. Global study of misinformation drivers and coping strategies. Cross cultural project in more than twenty countries examining how people interpret, resist, and mitigate health misinformation.

Image represents the data intensive, collaborative, and digitally mediated nature of contemporary health information work.

Scientometrics, altmetrics, and scholarly communication

Quantitative and mixed methods research on how scholarly work circulates, is evaluated, and shapes policy, including altmetrics and global inequalities in impact.

Altmetrics Impact assessment Global South and North
  • 2013. Garfield Award winning dissertation. Doctoral research recognized for contributions to understanding the effectiveness of science using web based metrics and citation analysis.
  • 2015–2021. Series of studies on altmetrics and policy impact. Examining how social media mentions, news coverage, and policy documents relate to traditional citation based impact.
  • Ongoing. AI and research quality. Large scale evaluation of how language models and AI tools intersect with peer review, research assessment, and perceptions of quality.

Many of these projects involve international collaborations and cross disciplinary teams, connecting information science, bibliometrics, and science policy.

Methods, theory, and cross cutting work

Methodological and theoretical contributions that support work in AI, health information, and scientometrics, including mixed methods designs and data infrastructures.

Mixed methods Qualitative and quantitative Data infrastructure
  • Methodology. Use of large scale social media data, survey research, experimental designs including EEG based studies, and qualitative coding of open ended responses.
  • Theory. Work draws on and contributes to frameworks from information behavior, human computer interaction, AI literacy, and science of science.
